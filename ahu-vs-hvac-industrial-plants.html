In the world of industrial engineering, terms like AHU and HVAC are often used interchangeably, but they represent very different things. What is an HVAC System?

HVAC stands for Heating, Ventilation, and Air Conditioning. It is the entire ecosystem that manages the air in your plant. An HVAC system includes the chillers, boilers, ductwork, control systems, and of course, the air handling units. With over 8 years of experience as an Industrial HVAC Contractor in Moradabad, we have designed full-scale systems for varied industrial applications.

What is an AHU (Air Handling Unit)?

The AHU is a specific component within the HVAC system. Think of it as the "lungs" of the building. It is the large metal box that contains the blower, heating or cooling elements, filter racks, and dampers. Frequently Asked Questions

Is an AHU the same as an HVAC system?

No, an Air Handling Unit (AHU) is a component of a larger HVAC system. While the HVAC system manages heating, ventilation, and air conditioning globally, the AHU specifically manages the air circulation and filtration within that system.

Do I need a double-skin AHU for my factory in UP?

Yes, for industrial environments in Uttar Pradesh, double-skin AHUs are recommended as they provide superior thermal insulation and prevent dust accumulation, which is critical for maintaining hygiene and energy efficiency.
